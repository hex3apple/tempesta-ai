STARTUP MONETIZES SANDWICH BITES AS NFTS 2.0; INVESTORS EAT IT UP

New Economy Built on Crumbs, Vibes, and Blockchain-Adjacent Hype

SAN FRANCISCO, CA — In the latest evolution of late-stage capitalism, Silicon Valley startup BiteChain has unveiled NFTs 2.0 — a next-gen marketplace where users can buy and trade digital representations of actual bites taken from sandwiches. Yes, you read that right. No, we’re not sure either.

The company calls them B.A.A.S. (Bites As A Service™) and claims it’s revolutionizing both lunch and ownership.

“This isn’t just about food,” said founder Theo Crunch, who wore three hoodies and the expression of someone who hasn’t slept since Dogecoin peaked. “It’s about experiencing the essence of a bite. The mouthfeel. The memory. The moment. We’ve digitized it.”

The Platform

Users upload pictures of their sandwich bites, which are then “immortalized” on the blockchain using BiteChain’s proprietary flavor-hashing algorithm. Each bite is tagged with:

  • Type of sandwich (e.g., “Artisanal Turkey Pesto on Sourdough”)
  • Bite angle (obtuse bites trade for higher value)
  • Crumb density
  • Emotional intention behind the chew

You don’t own the sandwich. You don’t eat the sandwich.
You own the vibe of having bitten it.

Investors Are All In

Venture capitalists threw $110 million at BiteChain before the app even had a Terms of Service. “We love businesses that don't make sense but sound disruptive in a pitch deck,” said VC Kevin D’Angelo while eating a sandwich he cannot afford to mint.

Applebee’s has already joined the pilot program, offering limited-edition bites of their “Triple Bacon Rodeo Crunch Wrap” as part of their “Late Capitalism Lunch Menu™.”

Critics Are… Spiraling

Economists, ethicists, and your cousin who majored in philosophy are deeply confused. “We’ve officially entered the era of Monetized Morsels,” said Dr. Lila Grossman, who has been screaming into a compost bin since the press release dropped.

One food justice advocate was quoted saying, “People can’t afford groceries and now we’re trading JPEGs of snacks?” while another simply said, “🫠.”

Still, The Numbers Are Ridiculous

A partially eaten Reuben sold for $87,000 after going viral on SandwichTok. One user flipped a digital bite of an egg salad croissant for a 600% profit after Elon Musk tweeted, “Kinda hungry now lol 🥪 #BAAS.”

The market is hot, weird, and impossible to explain at dinner parties.

What You Should Do Right Now

  • Take pictures of your lunch like it's stock in a startup
  • Say “bite equity” with a straight face
  • Claim your sandwich has historical value even if it’s from 7-Eleven
  • Prepare to cry when you realize your real sandwich has no resale value

Update: Meta Announces MetaMunch™—Full VR Sandwich Eating Experience

Next year, users will be able to simulate chewing on famous bites in the metaverse, including:

  • JFK’s last pastrami
  • Beyoncé’s Coachella grilled cheese
  • And “The Existential Nibble,” a sourdough crust eaten in total silence by Steve Jobs in 2007

At this point, we don’t know if the economy is collapsing or just evolving into a snack.

Either way: get in while it’s crunchy.
