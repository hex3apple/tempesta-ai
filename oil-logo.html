OIL GIANT UNVEILS ECO-FRIENDLY LOGO; CLIMATE CRISIS THANKS THEM FOR THE PNG

"We Spared No Expense On The Green Gradient," Says CEO

HOUSTON, TX — In a move hailed as a "giant leap for branding," oil conglomerate PetroMondo unveiled a new eco-friendly logo Monday, featuring a green leaf and a subtle gradient. "We spared no expense on the green gradient," said CEO Linda Slick. "This PNG alone will offset at least three decades of emissions—visually, anyway."

Environmentalists were quick to praise the logo's "bold use of transparency" and "commitment to the PNG format," though they noted the company’s actual emissions remain unchanged. PetroMondo’s new slogan, "Think Green, Drill Deeper," will appear on all company tankers, stationary, and apology letters.

"We believe this logo will inspire the next generation to recycle their feelings about oil," said Slick, before unveiling a commemorative reusable tote bag made from 100% recycled press releases.

      New Leaf Icon Said To Offset 400 Million Tons of Guilt

      HOUSTON, TX — In a bold move toward “a more sustainable visual identity,” oil conglomerate GigaPetro Global unveiled a new eco-friendly logo today, swapping its flaming oil barrel symbol for a soft green leaf with a little dew on it.

      “It’s the same planet-destroying fuel,” said GigaPetro CEO Lorne Vance, “but now it’s got, like, rounded corners.”

      The new logo — which cost $47 million to design, animate, and gently fade into B-roll footage of wind turbines the company does not own — was released alongside a sleek press kit made entirely of recycled buzzwords.

      “We Hear the Planet. We Just Don’t Want to Stop.”

      In a pre-recorded video, Vance explained that GigaPetro is committed to “honoring the climate conversation” while “continuing to provide the fossil fuels people need to ignore it.”

      “We know we’ve contributed to global emissions,” Vance said, standing in front of a Tesla while a flare stack burned softly in the background. “But with this new branding, we feel confident that we now look like we care.”

      Environmental Impact of the Rebrand

      According to GigaPetro’s in-house scientists (a.k.a. former Axe Body Spray chemists), the logo reduces environmental backlash by up to 70% on social media and 100% among people who get their climate news from airport ads.

      The press release claims the new logo is:

      • 100% compostable (as a PNG file)
      • Rendered in “carbon-neutral gradients”
      • Designed by “emotionally sustainable artists”

      Greenwashing? No, They Prefer “Eco-Aesthetics”

      Critics immediately slammed the rebrand as textbook greenwashing. GigaPetro responded by changing its LinkedIn bio to:

      “🌱 Fueling the future, softly.”

      A leaked internal memo revealed potential future slogans, including:

      • “Oil, but it feels organic”
      • “Crude with a conscience”
      • “From extraction to compassion™”

      Climate Crisis Reacts

      When asked for comment, the Earth responded with three simultaneous hurricanes and a heat wave in Greenland. An iceberg was spotted slow-clapping.

      Greta Thunberg reportedly sighed so hard it shifted the jet stream.

      What You Should Do Right Now

      • Adjust your thermostat out of spite
      • Drink out of your reusable bottle with judgmental intensity
      • Photoshop the new GigaPetro logo onto a burning forest
      • Ask your gas station if their vibes are “sustainably harvested”

      Update: GigaPetro Wins Award for “Most Emotionally Greener-Looking” Website

      The homepage includes auto-playing whale sounds and a quote from Rumi. The site loads slower than the actual transition to clean energy, but that’s part of the experience.

      At the end of the day, nothing says “we care” quite like replacing a flaming skull with a PNG leaf.
